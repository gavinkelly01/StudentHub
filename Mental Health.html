Why is your mental health important?

Your mood is entirely dependent on your mental health, without good mental health, your mood is low, your energy isn't there and life almost becomes a chore. Mental health dictates everything you feel, be it your mood, how you react to things or just how you go about your day, your mental health dictates it all.

What can affect your mental health

Really everything can, from experiences you've been through, to day-to-day life, to really anything that affects your emotions. Specifically for college students, the stress of the academic year and the pressure that comes with it can do a lot to negatively affect mental health. From exam stress, to assignment deadlines and just the constant pressure of college is the largest cause of mental health problems amongst college students, aswell as that, for many college students who are just becoming adults, substances and events where the comsumption of alcohol and other substances are done can be frequent, this can also lead to a downturn in mental health due to the affects those substances can have on your body and brain. Adversly, college students have many ways to improve their mental health, colleges offer many social events and options, socialising can vastly improve mental health and help alleviate the pressure put on by academics. Social life, time away from work and breaks in general can do a lot for someone's mental health, allowing for time for one's brain to rest and recupierate, allieviating stress and anxiety from someone's mental state.

How can I help with improve my mental health?

There are many ways you can improve your health, as previously mentioned, you can take breaks and spend time socially to help relax any tension, stress or anxiety you may be feeling. Now, this is only for people dealing with stress and not dealing with actual mental issues, methods such as meditiation, social interaction and relaxation are great for people who wish to burn away stress, but, for people dealing with mental health issues such as depression, anxiety or whatever else they may be dealing with, there are more serious and professional ways of dealing with what they are dealing with. For people battling mental health problems, there are many ways to get help, such as therapists, call/help lines or places to go for help. Below there are links to websites where you can go to seek help. Now, therapy sounds scary, pouring emotions to someone you don't know, which is why below the links will bring you to places that have hundreds of different people to contact, to try and find the right person for you, mental health is a serious, crucial part of somebody's life, and finding the right person to talk to in order to keep it in check is imperatively important.

I feel stuck mentally, what can I do?

It may feel sometimes like you're stuck in life when your mental health is down, depression, anxiety and stress can make you feel alone, scared and caught in a loop, you aren't. Mental health is a battle everybody goes through, and because of that there is so much information out there to help, millions of peoples stories that can help you, healing from poor mental health isn't quick, it isn't easy, but that's ok. It's normal to feel stuck when your mental health has declined but it isn't ok to stay stuck, the first step in healing is making the changes needed in your life, whether that be getting into a routine that involves helping yourself mentally, or finding a new environment to help you heal. Being social is massive for mental health, having the right people around you can slowly but surely impact your mental health positively and make the path a lot better. There are people out there who will help you, both professionals and non-professionals, both in person and online, feeling stuck is something many people go through, you are not alone.

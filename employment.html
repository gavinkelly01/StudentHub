Working while in college

Working part-time while in college can be challenging. In this article we talk about balancing study's with part-time work.

Balancing college and a job.

Attending college can be very expensive with most college tuition fees costing up to €3000. while there are many student that are entitled to grants there are many that are not and therefore a part-time job is necessary.

Here are some tips to balance the college and job lifestyle.

1.prioritize and plan

Make a planner. Have a planner containing your work hours and your classes. with this you can make dedicated study times during the week. It is also important to add deadlines to the planner to ensure that you will not fall behind schedule.

It is also useful to set prioritizes to determine what tasks are most important to tackle first. for example if there is an assignment that is due in a week and one that is due in 2 weeks the one that is due in a week should have higher priority.

2.Communicate with Employers.

Let your employers know that you are in college and are not available for the entire week. share the times that you are available to work with your employers so that your college and study times do not overlap with work. Ask for weekend days off from time to time, this will be a nice break in the weekend that you can use to relax or to study.

3.Take care of yourself.

We know that it can be very hard to look after yourself when you are so busy, however it is very important or else you will burn yourself out. You should aim to have 7-8 hours of sleep each night, maintain a healthy diet and exercise regularly this can be going to the gym or even getting out and going for a walk.

How to prepare for a job interview

Job interviews can make you very nervous, it is very important to be prepared for them.

Know what type of interview you will have.

When you get offered a job interview find out what style of interview it is such as a video call, telephone or a traditional in person meeting.

Research.

Before the interview you must research the role that you are applying for and the company that you have applied for. research things such as the companys history and the products and services that they provide. You must also have a great understanding of the role you are going for. Memorise your own CV, this is important as the interviwer will most likely ask you questions based on your CV.

Be yourself.

We all know that an interview is very stressful however we need to try our best to remain calm. By doing your research you should feel much more relaxed going into an interview. perfecting your CV

You need to have a well written CV when applying for a job.

Determine the Suitability of the Role

Use keywords that are associated with the job role that you are applying for. For the job that you are applying for look at the required skills and qulifactions and you will see if the jobs suits you.If the keywords on the job role don't fit into your CV the job may not be suited for you.

CV layout.

Ensure that the CV is no longer then 2 pages. We recommend that you make a CV in word and save it as a PDF and make the name of the file your name. The font should be consistant we recommend times new roman size 12 and headings to be size 14 or 16. Using bold and underline for important information is a nice touch.
